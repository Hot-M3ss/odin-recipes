Pea Soup

Description

This soup is known in France as Potage Saint-Germain, a suburb in Paris.

This recipe is a simple, healthy, and tasty treat and is best served with a side of bread and a garnish of shallots, and a cool -- not cold -- glass of water.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 medium shallots, finely chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 cups fresh shelled green peas
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons whipping cream (Optional)

Steps

  1. Melt the butter in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Cook the shallots until soft and translucent, about 3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the water and peas, season to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring to a boil
  5. then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until the peas are tender, 12 to 18 minutes.
  6. Puree the peas in a blender or food processor in batches.
  7. Strain back into the saucepan, stir in the cream if using, and reheat.
  8. Season to taste with salt and pepper before serving.
